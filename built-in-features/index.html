PHPfox has support for both Themes and Styles. A Theme control the basic layout and structure of your site. Themes are mainly made up off HTML template files. Each Theme can have as many Styles. Styles control the colors, fonts, font sizes; basically the CSS and icons for your site. Since you can make changes to your theme and styles direct from the AdminCP you will never have to worry about upgrading to a new version of ours as we never overwrite your changes. We only overwrite our own files on the server but never your changes.

Importing a theme or style requires uploading the theme to your server and then clicking the "Install" link from the theme manager.

With just these 2 files you could give your site an entirely new look.

When creating an announcement you can decide if your users can close and hide the announcement so they won't have to keep seeing it, however you can disable this feature for them in case you have some very important information that they must see.

Account Privacy

Invisible Mode

Your users can enable the "Invisible Mode" option from the "Privacy Settings" page. If enabled they will not be shown within any "Online" lists.

Profile Privacy

Users can customize how other users or guests interact with them via their profile.

Items

Control your default privacy for items you add on the site. Learn more about privacy control here.

Notifications

Users can control what email notifications are sent out to them. We send out emails to your users based on any activity related to items they add or are subscribed to.

Blocking Users

We give the ability for your users to block other users on the site. They can manage these users from their "Privacy Settings" page and remove them at anytime.

Newsletter

Internal PM or Email

You can send out a newsletter to your members either as an internal private message or an email.

Archive Support

You can archive each newsletter that is sent out.

Privacy Override

Your users may have a privacy setting enable to not receive a PM or an email, however you as an Admin may have some very important information that they must be aware off and you can override these privacy settings. This option is disabled by default.

Target Viewers

When you write a newsletter you can define who the target viewers are. You can select which user group and if the user is located in a specific country and what their gender is as well as their age group.

Newsletters per Round

If you have a lot of users it might be too much to ask your server to send all those emails at once. Not to worry you can define how many emails to send per a pages refresh. We set this to 50 by default.

HTML & Plain Text Emails

When you create your newsletter and you plan on sending out an email you can add both HTML and Plain Text content.

Failsafe

If you can't send out all the newsletters in one session you can always come back at a later time, even days later and you can continue sending out your newsletter.

Subscriptions

Monetize

You can create subscription packages that can connect to a user group with special "powers" other user groups don't have. You can even charge for your users to be part of these special groups.

Pay Before Usage

You can include subscription packages and force new users to sign up for a package before they can officially use the site. Once their payment is "okayed" their account will automatically be updated and they will be notified that they can now enjoy your community.

Easy Management for End Users

Your users can easily manage their subscription purchases and keep track of any past purchases they have made and if they plan on upgrading to another membership level direct from their dashboard.

Payment Gateways API

We have included with our product support for both PayPal and 2checkout giving you the best of what both companies can provide for online payments.

Custom Fields

Custom Groups

For each custom field you create it can belong to a custom group. By splitting up your fields into groups it will make it a lot easier on your members to edit these fields.

Custom Field Location

You can define where a custom field should be located when its being displayed on a users profile. It can be on either the left or right panels or within the "Basic Information" block.

Required Fields

You may want certain fields to require a value when a user registers or edits their profile.

Include on Registration

You can add each custom field to the registration form.

Supported Types

We support the following custom fields... Large Text Area Small Text Area (255 Characters Max) Selection Multiple Selection Radio Checkbox

Editing Custom Fields

Your users can edit the values for your custom fields from their "Edit Profile" section. All the custom fields are placed there and neatly grouped for easy and quick access.

Inline Profile Editing

Your users can edit their custom fields from the "Edit Profile" section or they can directly do this from their profile using our "Inline Profile Editing" feature, which allows them to update each field on the spot.

Emoticons

Emoticon Packages

You can easily upload a new emoticon package. It does not even have to be intended for PHPfox, all it needs is to have all the emoticon image archived into a ZIP and we will unzip and do the rest for you.

Emoticon Manager

Using our emoticon manager you can easily activate or deactivate an emoticon package. You can also edit, delete or download a ZIP archive for each package. When managing an emoticon package you can change the symbol representing an emoticon as well as define the order for the emoticons that will be used when displaying them to your members.

Overall Site Support

In most areas that provide larger content like a comment, blog entry or a shoutbox message we give your users the ability to use emoticons. In most areas that support emoticons we provide a text editor where users can see all the supported emoticons and choose which ones they want to use.

Multilingual

Create Your Own Language Package

From the AdminCP you can create your own language package and translate it into any language you would like. You can select what language to create the language package from, which by default is the default English language package. We provide support for text direction (Left to Right or Right to Left).

Phrase Manager

Easily translate your language package direct from our powerful phrase manager.

Import/Export Language Packages

You can import and export language packages direct from your Language Manager. Importing a language package requires uploading the language files to your and clicking on the "Install" link. You can find already translated Language Package here.

Allow User Selection

For each language package you can define if your users can use this language package publicly.

Report Users/Items

Reporting Items

On most items that are added to the site by your users we give the community the ability to report the item to Admins in case they feel it breaches your sites terms and/or conditions.

Reporting Users

Your users can report other members if they feel they have something on their profile or have done something on the site that breaches your terms and/or conditions.

AdminCP Report Notices

When you visit your AdminCP we provide you with information if anything has been reported to you or your staff can look over the report right away.

Managing Report Items

We group all the reported items within the AdminCP so you or your staff can look over each reported item and deal with them appropriately.

Custom Categories

When your users report an item they can select what category the report belongs too. This will make it easier for you and your staff to understand why the item was reported. You can create as many categories as you want. We provide the following categories by default... Attacks Individual or Group Copyright Infringement Drug Use Nudity or Pornography Violence

SEO Friendly

SEF (Search Engine Friendly) URLs

All items that search bots can have access too uses our SEF URLs. An example of our SEF URLs we could link to a blog using its unique ID number like this... http...//www.yoursite.com/index.php?cmd=blog&id=1234 Instead we use the title of the item, which in this case is the title of the blog entry to create the unique identifier for us. The URL would look like this instead... http...//www.yoursite.com/blog/the-title-of-this-blog/

Unique Meta Keywords & Description

When viewing certain sections we provide you with options in the AdminCP to give that section custom meta keywords and/or descriptions. When viewing an item like a blog or photo or even a users profile we make sure we create unique meta keywords and/or descriptions based on the items title or description.

H1 Breadcrumbs

We use breadcrumb standards when users browse your community and always use the H1 tags for search bots to pick up the important keywords for that specific page.

Keyword String Removal

There are certain words that you may not want to be included within your sites keywords and we give you the option to control these keywords.
